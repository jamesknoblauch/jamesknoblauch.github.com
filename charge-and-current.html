Charge and Current

Sun 17 March 2013 by James Knoblauch

Definitions

Electric charge, q, is a physical property of matter that causes it to experience a force when near other electrically charged matter. Charge is measured in coulombs (C).

Elementary charge, e, is the charge of a single electron,

e=-1.602×10 -19C

Electric current, i, is the rate of change of charge, with respect to time, measured in amperes (A).

Direct current (DC) is a current that remains constant with time, so di dt=0.

Alternating current (AC) is a current that changes sinusoidally with time, so di dt≠0.

Mathematical Relationship

For current i and charge q,

i= dq dt

Q is the total charge transferred from time t 0 to t.

Q=∫ t 0 t idt

Units

1A=1 C s
