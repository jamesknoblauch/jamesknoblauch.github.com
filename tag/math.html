Power

 Sun 17 March 2013 by James Knoblauch

Definitions

Power, p, is the rate of change of energy with respect to time, measured in watts (W).

Energy, w, is the capcity to do work, measured in Joules (J)

Mathematical Relationship

For energy w and time t,

p= dw dt

Relation to voltage and current,

p= dw dt= dw dq⋅ dq dt=vi
w=∫ t 0 tpdt=∫ t 0 tvidt

Units

1W=1 J s

Sign Conventions

Passive Sign Convention

If the current enters an element through the positive teminal,

p=+vi

If the current enters through the negative terminal,

p=-vi

Active Sign Convention

If the current enters an element through the positive teminal,

p=-vi

If the current enters through the negative terminal,

p=+vi

Conservation

Power Absorbed = -Power Supplied

The algebraic sum of power in a circuit is zero.

∑ p=0
